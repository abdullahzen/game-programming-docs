1. After extraction is complete, import all your assets into your Unity Project.
    - ![image](https://docs.unity3d.com/560/Documentation/uploads/Main/ImportPackageMenu.png)
    - Click on `Import Package` -> `Custom Packages..`
    - Select all three assets `.zip` files you downloaded earlier
    > By default, textures may not be attached to the models as materials when you import external assets. You may need to drag and drop the `.jpg` or `.png` images of the textures imported from your project view in the lower pane to the corresponding model in the hierarchy pane (left pane).
4. Now you should have the building blocks to start the task.
5. Create a mini bowling game with these sepcifications:
    - Pins should rest at the end of a bowling alley
    - Ability to throw the ball on the bowling alley towards the pins
    - The ball should roll and rotate when sliding on the bowling alley
    - The ball should tumble bins it collides with
    - Make it as realistic as in real life
    - Different balls with different colors should have different weights (masses)
    - Physics must be taken into account. i.e a higher mass ball effect on the pins should be higher than a lower mass ball.
> For inputs to control the ball or if you want to map a button to trigger the throw, you may consult the following example: https://www.codegrepper.com/code-examples/csharp/unity+spacebar+input
